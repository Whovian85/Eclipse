OUR GOALS

We want to live and work in a world where high-quality crops, clean water, closed loop economies, and renewable sources of energy are a shared way of life for industry and consumers. Most of all, we want a world with dramatically lowered carbon emissions and a secure climate.

After a decade of delivering strong sustainability results, in March 2018, we were proud to announce our most ambitious set of sustainability goals ever to help realize this world. Our 2025 goals are focused on four, vital SDG areas where we – in partnership with our suppliers, NGOs, governements, and others – can most move the world forward:

CLIMATE ACTION

Our 2025 goal: 100% of our purchased electricity will be from renewable sources and a 25% GHG emissions reduction across our full value chain

Brewing and distributing beer require intense amounts of electricity and energy. So do many of our value chain processes, from growing barley, to cooling products at the point-of-sale or in consumers’ homes. Managing energy usage and emissions brings benefits to every business including efficiencies, mitigation of risks, and increases effectiveness in process. Our focus on energy efficiency and Greenhouse Gas Emissions (GHG) reduction is both external and internal and we are continuously seeking to improve the efficiency of our operations, through the use of cleaner fuels, reduced fuel consumption, and shift to eco-friendly coolers.

Join our climate action work by adopting goals or targets around:

• Set a Science-Based Target of emissions across your full value chain using climate science • Absolute: Reduce GHG emission 10% year over year
• Absolute: Reduce GHG emissions by 20% by 2025
• Intensity: Reduce GHG emissions per tonnage (or revenue) by 50% by 2030

SMART AGRICULTURE

Our 2025 goal: 100% of our farmers will be skilled, connected, and financially empowered.

We rely on the highest-quality grains and crops to make the best beers in the world. Barley is the primary grain in beer, and as the biggest purchaser of barley, it is our focal crop. Our other brewing crops include rice, corn, hops, sorghum and cassava. We source from more than 50,000 farmers globally, and our 180 agronomists work alongside them in fields around the world to promote smart, sustainable agricultural practices.

Our goal takes a grower-focused approach. We provide growers access to the tools, resources and partnerships they need to improve crop productivity, enhance their livelihoods, and protect natural eco-systems. In particular, we invest in partners and technology that resolve water stress, soil and climate risks, infrastructure challenges, and human rights violations.

• Water usage reduction
• Carbon emission reduction
• Improving smallholder income
• Development programs with your farmers
• Increasing livelihood of local farmers

CIRCULAR PACKAGING

Our 2025 goal: 100% of our product will come in primary packaging that is returnable or made from majority recycled content

We have an important role to play in driving a more circular world. Today, 43% of our volume is packaged in returnable glass bottles, which are 8x less carbon-intensive than one-way glass bottles. Through our brands and recovery programs in local markets, we work to defend the returnable system and promote it with consumers.

Additionally, we are constantly looking for ways to increase the recycled material in our packaging. We support the recovery and re-use of glass, aluminum, and PET, in close collaboration with suppliers and partners. Similarly, we welcome innovations that light-weight our containers or eliminate waste in our verticalized operations.

Packaging is also one of the most significant components of our carbon footprint. While 99% of our packaging globally is recyclable, we have a far way to go in closing the full loop and reducing emissions across the lifespan of our products.

Join our circular packaging work by adopting goals or targets around:

• Increase recycled content in one-way packaging through collaboration
• Increase supply of recycled content through post-consumer waste recovery programs
• Promote and protect returnable packaging systems
• Reduce the amount of material in our packaging
• Reduce carbon footprint through recycled content and returnable packaging
• Explore the use of alternative materials, technologies and designs for adverse materials
